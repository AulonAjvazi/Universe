Our Universe

What is our Universe

  1. Definition: The universe is everything that exists, including all matter, energy, space, and time.
  2. Components: It contains galaxies, stars, planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and cosmic dust.
  3. Structure: The universe is vast and largely empty, with galaxies grouped in clusters and superclusters.
  4. Expansion: The universe is expanding, meaning galaxies are moving away from each other over time.
  5. Age: The universe is estimated to be about 13.8 billion years old, originating from the Big Bang.
  6. Dark Matter and Dark Energy: Most of the universe's mass-energy content is made up of dark matter (which we can't see) and dark energy (which is driving its expansion).
